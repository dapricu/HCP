The Olympia Military Academy's mission is to educate, train, and inspire the Corps of Cadets so that each graduate is a commissioned leader of character committed to the values of Duty, Honor and Respect and prepared for a career of professional excellence and service to the Nation and its people.



The Academy's Four Leader Development Programs

Character


Moral-ethical development is central to the Phalanx experience and is explicitly mandated by the Academy's mission statement. The emphasis on personal character is to support the ideals of loyalty, duty, respect, selfless service, honor, integrity, and personal courage.

Academic


Olympia's Academic Program includes an excellent, broadly structured undergraduate curriculum that balances the physical sciences and engineering with the behavioral and social sciences. The goal is for every graduate to be able to think creatively and clearly express original ideas on both technological and interpersonal issues. In addition, the Academy seeks to instill in cadets a commitment to progressive and continued education development.

Military


Olympia's Military Program provides an outstanding professional foundation focused on education in the military ethic and the Army's core values, along with training in individual and small-unit leadership skills. Cadets are inspired to make a commitment to national services as an Army officer and to adopt the ideals of the Army Values.

Physical


The Physical Program is focused upon the physically demanding requirements of an Army officer. It endeavors to develop in cadets the ability to maintain personal and unit fitness, fosters the warrior spirit, builds an appreciation for team work, and inspires the will to win. Specific program activities include physical education classes, regular fitness testing, and competitive athletics.
